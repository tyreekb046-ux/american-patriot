Behind the Border Bill: What the Senate Vote Really Means for Immigration Policy

The passage of the Secure Borders Act represents a seismic shift in how Washington approaches immigration enforcement. For the first time in decades, a bipartisan supermajority has endorsed a framework that prioritizes physical barriers, technological surveillance, and expedited removal proceedings over the catch-and-release policies that have defined border management for years.

But the real story isn't just about the vote itself — it's about what happens next. Legal challenges are already being prepared by advocacy groups who argue that several provisions of the bill violate due process protections.
